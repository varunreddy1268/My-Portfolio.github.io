BirdClef - 2022 Kaggle Competition

Associated with San Jose State University

The BirdClef is a kaggle competition challenge that is a bioacoustic monitoring assignment that aims to provide a passive, low-labor, and cost-effective technique for analyzing endangered bird populations through their birdcalls. Species diversity and movement are monitored in almost all conservation initiatives. Long-term acoustic data analysis is essential for these efforts, but it necessitates complex algorithms that must adapt to changing environmental conditions.

The goal is to develop a machine learning model to identify bird species by sound. The input is continuous audio data and the output is to recognize the species. The main challenge is to train reliable classifiers with limited training data.

Watch The Road

Associated with San Jose State University

Distracted driving can easily cause accidents. According to National Highway Transportation and Safety Administration (NHTSA), distracted driving is causing about 3000 deaths and 280,000 injuries per year. Distracted driving has become all too common these days with advent of smart phones and social media.

We believe that computer vision based solutions can be effectively used to detect inattentive drivers using any sort of dashboard cameras and alert drivers.
